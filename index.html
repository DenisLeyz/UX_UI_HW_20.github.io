HomesFirst

Website redesign for a charity
organization that helps 10 000+
homeless people in Toronto.

Project type: website redesign
Team: 4 people
Responsibilities: user research, wireframes, animations
Tools used: Miro, Figma, InVision

The Problem?

HomesFirst website was ineffective in eliciting empathy and trust, and persuading people to join its' cause.

Frankly, everything from the website’s organization to it’s colour scheme required major adjustments.

Step 1:

Research

Our First step was to contact HomesFirst representatives, preferablly someone in an executive position, to find our more information on the organization. After a number of unsuccessful attempts, we decided to proceed with the project regardless, and create our vision for the website.

We kicked off our project by conducting 1-on-1 interviews as well as administering an online survey to Toronto community memebers. Our goal? To find out peoples atittudes towards homelessness in Toronto, homeless people, and donating to this cause.

In addition, we conducted a competitor analysis to examine what the local competition is doing (both right and wrong).

Our interviews indicated that people consider homelessness a very big problem in Toronto. Some try to help the homeless whenever they can, but quite a few that donating money on the street is not an effecting way of helping the homeless.

Survey results indicated that 40% of respondents did not donate to charities for various reasons, including not believing that one’s funds will not be spent effectively, and not being persuaded enough to donate.

The results of our competitor analysis indicated that HomesFirst lacks in functionality compared to other similar organizations in Toronto. We have identified a number of worthy functions to be included in our site, as well as the competitors’ shortcomings.

Most noteworthy, it was almost a staple for any charity website to have a highly visible and usable Donation CTA. HomesFirst seriously lacked in that department, and it was clear from the start.

Step 2:

Research analysis

Once we had all of our data, we began to analyze it. First, we came up with a definitive User Persona to guide us withour ideation process.

To make sense of our finidngs, we wrote out all of our interview insights and grouped them into similar themes.

We used them along with the survey data and the competitor analysis to come up with a number of potential suggestible improvements ussing the “I Like, I Wish, What If?” method.

We voted on the best ideas in each category. Every suggestion that received at least 1 vote was placed on a Prioritization Matrix to identify most important ones.

We also created a Journey map. By visualizing the process of a user visiting HomesFirst website, we were able to find pain points that a user may experience. It also helped us to build our Site Map.

Step 3:

Ideation

One of the team members created a site map. Sitemap allowed us to visualize the layout of our site and get started on User Flows.

Speaking of user flows, I was put in charge of creating them.

The first version came out confusing and clumsy, but it got the message across.

We focused on engaging the community through donations and volunteering.

Rounds of iterations made important functions streamlined.


The goal was to make donations accessible from any point of the page, make the CTA’s both visible to direct.


I wanted people to make flexible donations (in terms of type, ammount,and other things) through a pop-up.


I believed it would increase conversion rates, as simplifying donation would make it more likely to people to go through with it.

With User Flows perfected, it was time for us to build a lo-fi wireframe.

Each of the team members made a rough mockup of the site the way he envisionned it. The team got together and went over each of the designs, pointing out things we would like to see on HomesFirst.

It was decided to collectively make a wireframe by drawing pleasing elemenets from everyone’s versions.

The wireframe on the left is what we came up with!

Usability Testing

Since we are UX professionals after all, we decided to not get preocupied with design until we run some usability tests to see whether the overall layout makes sense, whether the users the page sections, the theme, and whether the IA makes sense.


We ran a total of 5 tests that indicated that overall we were moving in the right direction. Yet, we realzied that some aspects of our website still required major adjustments.



These improvements are discussed in the upcoming section.

Step 4:

Iterations

First things first, our IA required some serious attention.


Up until now it was remained mostly untouched. Our results, however, indicated that some things are not well groups, so the team performed a cord sorting excercise, and updated sitemap.

Then, making our way down, we began iterating the header. We wanted to give more attention to the “I Need Help” CTA and differentiate it from the rest of the menu.

The Hero Image section recieved a total of 4 rounds of iterations.


We wanted to have a captivating, emotionally-eliciting image coupled with a clearly visible CTA.


We decided that black and white background successfully manages to maintain a highly emotional appeal while not taking attention away from the rest of the section.

Buttons recieved minor tweaks.

We added icons to make CTAs stand out more, and swithced out the font for a better one.

Our statistic boxes section recieved a lot of love because thought it could be pivotal in capturing user’s attention, building trust and increasing awareness about both homelessness in Toronto and organization.


I proposed to make these boxes interactive - you could hover on them to uncover some other type of information.


We decided that providing annual statistics of the organization’s activity activity first, and then switching it with much bigger numbers that reflect the true size of the homelessness problem.

Step 5:

What's next?

Unfortunately, this project came to an end with us failing to ever get in contact with HomesFirst. It is unfortunate, because we believe they could have benefited from our redesign, and maybe even incorporated some of our proposed features into their website.

For HomesFirst, if they ever decide to take up our redesign idea, the next step would be creating “Volunteer” and “About” pages.“About” page in particular is inportant for building trust, as it provides a look inside the organization.

I learned a lot about designing with the purpose of elicitingvery specific reactions. It is no easy task, especially because a creator can never be a user. Additionally, I became more aware of CTAs, specifically I became better at organizing layouts to emphasize CTAs whenever needed.
